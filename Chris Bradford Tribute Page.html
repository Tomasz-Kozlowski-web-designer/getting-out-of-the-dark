Chris Bradford

Not only an award-winning writer

Literary career

Chris Bradford achieved international success thanks to the Young Samurai series which tells the story of an English boy Jack Fletcher, who is shipwrecked off the coast of Japan in 1611. Fortunately, he is adopted by a samurai sword-master Masamoto Takeshi sends him to a renowned samurai school. The series describes Jack's life, his search for allies and deadly fights with sworn enemies in the 17th-century Japan. The 10-book series (2019) is a great source of knowledge about the Japanese culture and history, since the books contain descriptions of historical places, clothes, customs, proverbs and weaponry etc.

Another successful series by Chris Bradford is Bodyguard, which is set in contemporary times. It tells the story of Connor Reeves, who at the age of 14 joins a professional group of bodyguards to protect the teenage children of VIPs. The series is an action-packed thriller.

Music career

Before he became a full-time writer Chris Bradford worked as a professional musician. He wrote songs, worked with Dave Calhoqoun, Iain Archer, Graham Gouldman and James Blunt. He even gave a concert for Queen Elizabeth II. In addition, he is an author of five non-fiction books on music.

Martial arts skills

Chris Bradford's books would never be so good without his expertise. At the age of 8 he started training judo, but later on he also practised many different fighting styles including: karate, kickboxing, samurai swordmanship and ninjutsu. The martial arts skills proved invaluable for the writing of Young Samuraiseries as they add authenticity to the fight scenes.

As regards Bodyguard Bradford went through a complete bodyguard course including among others such skills as unarmed combat, defensive driving and surveillance. He is a Member of the British Bodyguard Association.
